National Parks

2016 marks the 100th anniversary of the founding of the National Park Service, a network of over 20,000 employees, volunteers and partners, tasked with protecting the National Parks. They offer stewardship of the parks, in their words "...engaging communities through recreation, conservation, and historic preservation programs."


The National Parks continue to grow in popularity, with a record 307 million visitors in 2015. There are 411 park sites spanning over 84 million acres of US land, highlighted by the 58 National Parks. This project showcases a primary way in which people engage with the parks: through photos. Photography of the National Parks, immortalized by Ansel Adams, is now often shown socially through Instagram. This project showcases the locations and patterns of park photography through thousands of Instagram posts.

Inside the Numbers

How did those 330,000 posts break down? From the original number, about 50% had location data. Those posts were filtered again to include only locations within the geographic boundaries of each park. That left us with 84,796 posts, roughly 25% of the original count. The breakdown per park is to the right.

There is actually a negative correlation between the parks 2015 rank in popularity and the number of posts that fit our filter. There's several factors that could have influenced these findings. One is reception - posts without GPS data wouldn't have made it here. National Parks tend to be remote areas, so potentially poor coverage should be taken into consideration. Another is season. Picking a finite amount of posts means that will stretch over different amounts of time per park. Below, you can see a sampling of one week's worth of posts to see how frequently certain parks are mentioned. For more detail about this, see "Technical Explanations".
